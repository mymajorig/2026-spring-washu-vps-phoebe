Phoebe's Interview

This interview examines how people perceive websites through logical versus creative lenses. Some users prioritize structure, clarity, and efficiency, while others focus on aesthetics, originality, and emotional impact. By exploring these differences, the study aims to better understand how design choices influence user experience and how websites can be made more intuitive and engaging for diverse thinking styles.

Creative Thinking

"creative thinking is divergent, imaginative, and 'right-brain' oriented, aiming to generate novel ideas, break patterns, and explore possibilities"

“When you land on a website, what is the first criterion you subconsciously evaluate?”

“How navigable it is. Normally I don't go on a website to just look at it, normally I go because I need something and I’m looking for something. I shouldn't have to go through 8 million steps to find the section that I need”

Do you interpret design elements symbolically (as expressive choices) or functionally (as usability tools)?

“It depends but mostly symbolically. I think if its color or background or something is very obviously decorative I think that it is symbolic. But when it comes to a toolbar or articles or links that they choose to showcase, I that it is more functional”

Can you recall a website where aesthetic design altered your emotional state or trust level? How?

“Literally today. I went onto booking.com and everything is so overwhelming on the front page. It’s neon yellow, there’s a city in the background there are words everywhere and it was so insanely overwhelming that I immediately left the website.”

When design deviates from norms, do you experience it as innovative or disorienting?

“Again, it depends. When it comes to more creative elements, I consider it innovative. When it comes to functionality, it’s like suddenly no one knows how to do it anymore and it becomes inconvenient.”
