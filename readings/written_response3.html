Writing Response 3

Prompt: What is a human-made creation that brings out the best in you? Why?

I cherish the slow process when it comes to my morning and night routines. Typically, people may rush to get up in the morning so they can arrive at class early or start their day early or rush in the night so they can get enough sleep for the next day. For me, going slow in both of these ways helps set up up for the next day and helps me wind down from the current day. I think the best way to live life is in a way where you maintain focused and secure on your goals. Having a slow unwind at night (ex. giving time to myself to reflect on the day and/or commit a low effort hobby of my choosing) allows not only my nervous system to calm down but it acts as a minor reset. It reminds me on different ways I can continue living life in my own way and centers me back to things I enjoy. Reflecting on the day contributes to helping me grow in life and in my pursuits and helps organize my goals. In the morning, taking time to wake up helps me slowly get introduced to the day without immediately being overwhelmed with tasks that may have to get done. It mitigates stress and anxiety and gives me time to ground myself before being hit with outside factors.
