Do Artifacts Have Ethics?

Three questions that stood out to me were “What will the use of this technology encourage me to notice?”, “What was required of the earth so that I might be able to use this technology?”, and ”What desires does the use of this technology dissipate?”. For the first question, I think it helps to get to the core of technology as the purpose of it is to encourage certain habits or actions. Even when the purpose of technology is to make something easier to do, the foundation of this motivation is to increase the popularity of the specific action. For example, developing better camera’s in iphones is an indirect encouragement for people to take more pictures or notice moments where a picture should be taken. The second question I chose is something that I often think about when using technology which is the environmental resources it consumes. Especially with the rise of AI usage, I think this is something people are starting to notice more than ever. The idea that human creation is not possible without the earth is factual but often overlooked, so I’m glad we are bringing the idea back into the light. Lastly, the third question stuck out to me, because I think one should also consider not just how technology improves the good things, but how it can get rid of the bad. Often times I feel that there is technology that is developed to improve lives in different ways; however, sometimes highlighting the good without ridding of the bad can cause the authentic effect of the product to be neutralized.
